"Every frame is a negotiation between light, patience, and the unpredictable wild."

I'm a 19-year-old CSE student at VIT Chennai who spends every break chasing wildlife across India's most iconic reserves — and beyond. Computer science teaches me systems — the wild teaches me everything else.

Armed with a Nikon Z system, I've stalked tigers through the grasslands of Jim Corbett, watched leopards dissolve into the Aravalli scrub at Sariska, tracked Bengal tigers across the ancient terrain of Ranthambore, scaled the ancient Lion Rock fortress of Sigiriya, and chased elephants and leopards through the dry scrub of Yala in Sri Lanka.

My photography isn't about perfect shots — it's about honest ones. The half-hidden, the backlit, the fleeting. Conservation starts with people caring, and people care about what they can see.

Away from the lens, I play guitar — since 8th grade. I've cleared Trinity College London Grade 4 and Rockschool Grade 2. Same discipline, different language.
